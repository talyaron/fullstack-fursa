Nazareth

Nazareth (Arabic: النَّاصِرَة; Hebrew: נָצְרַת) is the largest city in the Northern District of Israel. Nazareth is known as "the Arab capital of Israel".

In the New Testament, the town is described as the childhood home of Jesus, and as such is a center of Christian pilgrimage, with many shrines commemorating biblical events.

Religious sites :

** Christian : Nazareth is home to dozens of monasteries and churches, many of them in the Old City.

1. The Church of the Annunciation is the largest Catholic church in the Middle East. In Roman Catholic tradition, it marks the site where the Archangel Gabriel announced the future birth of Jesus to Mary

2. The Basilica of Jesus the Adolescent, run by the Salesian religious order, at the top of the hill overlooking the city from the north

** Muslim : Muslim holy sites and places of worship include.

1. Ela-Nabi Sa'in Mosque is a mosque located at a height of 487 meters above sea level, on the northwestern outskirts of Nazareth.

2. The White Mosque is the oldest mosque in Nazareth, and is located in Harat Alghama or the "Mosque Quarter" in the center of Nazareth's Old Market.

Mary's Well

Mary's Well is reputed to be located at the site where, according to the Catholic tradition, Angel Gabriel appeared to Mary, mother of Jesus and announced that she would bear the Son of God – an event known as the Annunciation.
